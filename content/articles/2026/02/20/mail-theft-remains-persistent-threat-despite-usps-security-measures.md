Mail theft continues to be a widespread problem across the United States, with criminals regularly targeting mailboxes for credit cards, checks, and other financial documents. Despite ongoing security initiatives by the U.S. Postal Service, the issue persists at significant levels.

The USPS has implemented various security measures to combat mail theft, though their effectiveness in fully eliminating the problem remains limited. Thieves targeting mail seek high-value items such as new credit cards, blank checks, and tax documents that can be used for fraudulent purposes or identity theft.

Financial experts and law enforcement agencies have emphasized the importance of individual protective measures to mitigate risk. These recommendations include monitoring mail delivery schedules, considering mail collection services, and promptly retrieving mail from residential mailboxes to reduce the window of opportunity for thieves.

Consumers are also advised to monitor their financial accounts regularly for unauthorized activity and consider placing holds on new credit accounts. Additional steps include using USPS Informed Delivery to track incoming mail and requesting that sensitive financial documents be sent electronically when possible.

The persistence of mail theft underscores the ongoing need for both institutional security improvements and individual vigilance in protecting financial information and assets.

- Mail thieves regularly target credit cards and checks from residential and commercial mailboxes
