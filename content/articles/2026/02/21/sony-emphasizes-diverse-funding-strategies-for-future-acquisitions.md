Sony Group Corp. is prioritizing a diversified approach to capital sourcing as it pursues strategic acquisitions, according to Chief Executive Officer Hiroki Totoki.

The executive emphasized that relying solely on traditional financing methods—bank debt and stock issuance—limits the company's flexibility in pursuing asset acquisitions. By tapping private equity firms and exploring alternative funding mechanisms, Sony aims to maintain greater strategic optionality when evaluating potential deals.

This broader capital strategy reflects a shift in how large technology and entertainment conglomerates approach M&A activity in an increasingly competitive landscape. Private equity partnerships and non-traditional funding sources can offer advantages including faster deal execution, reduced dilution to existing shareholders, and access to specialized investment networks.

Totoki's remarks suggest Sony is preparing for continued acquisition activity, though the company has not announced specific targets. The diversified funding approach allows Sony to move quickly when opportunities arise while managing its balance sheet more strategically.
